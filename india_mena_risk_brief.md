# India-MENA Strategic Risk Brief

## Key judgments
1. India is exposed to MENA instability through energy imports, shipping routes, diaspora safety, trade corridors and diplomatic balancing.
2. Red Sea and Gulf shipping disruption can increase logistics cost and affect energy-sensitive sectors.
3. Escalation risk should be monitored through attacks on shipping, sanctions signals, diplomatic breakdown and energy price volatility.

## Exposure channels
- Crude oil and LNG imports
- Maritime shipping and insurance cost
- Indian diaspora in Gulf countries
- Remittance flows
- Trade and construction contracts
- Strategic diplomatic relations

## Monitoring indicators
- Shipping incidents near chokepoints
- Energy price spikes
- Embassy advisories
- Sanctions announcements
- Military mobilisation signals
- Diplomatic negotiations

## Scenario view
- Base case: contained disruption and periodic volatility.
- Downside case: shipping and energy shock.
- Severe case: regional escalation affecting diaspora, trade and energy security.

## Implications
Businesses and policy teams should maintain contingency planning for logistics, energy cost, workforce safety and regional exposure.
