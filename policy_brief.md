# Policy Brief: AI-Enabled Digital Governance for Faster Public Service Delivery

## Executive summary
Public-service delivery delays arise from manual verification, fragmented data, weak SLA enforcement, and limited citizen feedback. An AI-enabled service delivery control tower can improve speed, transparency and accountability if deployed with strong governance safeguards.

## Problem diagnosis
- Applications move across departments without real-time visibility.
- Citizens lack clear status updates.
- Grievances are triaged manually.
- Escalation happens late.
- Performance is reviewed after delays have already occurred.

## Policy options

| Option | Benefit | Risk | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Manual SLA review | Low cost | Slow, reactive | Not sufficient |
| Central grievance cell | Better accountability | Still manual | Use as base layer |
| AI-assisted triage + dashboard | Faster routing and monitoring | Bias, data quality risk | Recommended with safeguards |

## Recommended solution
Create an AI-enabled grievance and service delivery control tower with:
- Automated application classification
- SLA risk alerts
- Citizen status notifications
- Escalation prediction
- Department-level KPI dashboards
- Human review for sensitive cases

## Safeguards
- Human-in-the-loop decisions
- Audit logs
- Bias testing
- Data minimisation
- Citizen appeal mechanism

## 100-day plan
- Day 0-30: Process mapping, data audit, KPI baseline
- Day 31-60: Build dashboard and triage prototype
- Day 61-90: Pilot in two departments
- Day 91-100: Review, refine and scale decision
